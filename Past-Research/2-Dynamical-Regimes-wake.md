# Dynamical Regimes of Cortical Activity during Wakefulness

Unique integrative properties of stochastic network states [Zerlaut & Destexhe, _Neuron_ (2017)](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1MebSSG-ec1b8Tl5dy28MNlHemhisDlcJ/view?usp=share_link)

This manuscript is a perspective written with my PhD supervisor inspired by the introduction and discussion of my PhD thesis.

Desynchronised brain states have been associated with wakefulness and high arousal states, but the mechanisms behind those network states and their functional advantages remain elusive. We suggest that neuronal networks displaying asynchronous irregular (AI) activity can implement a low-level form of awareness, due to their specific responsiveness properties. We emphasise the importance of the conductance state and stochasticity to explain these properties. We suggest that the purpose of cortical structures is to generate AI states with optimal responsiveness, to be globally aware of external stimuli.


This study is the main result of my postdoctoral work at IIT. Back in 2017, I was amazed by the new reports showing the rich dynamics of the cerebral cortex during wakefulness in rodents. Focusing on the non-rhythmic states (i.e. excluding the low frequency rhythms of very low arousal / sleep), I was looking for a minimal network model able to generate such diversity.

We demonstrated that randomly connected spiking networks with moderate recurrence strength exhibit a spectrum of asynchronous states, from afferent-dominated regimes (AD regimes, characterised by sparse activity) to recurrent-dominated regimes (RD regimes, characterised by dense activity and balanced synaptic currents). The model predicted a set of specific neurophysiological signatures for the different network states that were all confirmed by intracellular recordings in the mouse sensory cortex. In addition, a theoretical analysis evidenced the computational specificity of each regime. The AD regime is optimal for the encoding of complex synaptic patterns while the RD regime sets a high sensitivity to incoming inputs. 

Those results _could provide the theoretical foundations to understand the link between the dynamics and function of cortical networks during wakefulness_ (see research project).
